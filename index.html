Renaissance Period Inspired Wine Label

Role: Lead Designer · Timeline: July-September 2025 · Tools: Illustrator

Overview

This project fulfilled the requirement to create a design contextualized within the Renaissance movement, applying its principles to a chosen product. The product selected was Label Design for a Wine Manufacturer, specifically for the winery's flagship Moulis-en-Médoc vintage harvested in 2006. The aim was to design a premium label, titled "Renaissance" (a wordplay on the historical movement and the designer's name), for the fictional vineyard, "The Glass of Gold." The design needed to visually convey the wine's quality (richness, intense black fruit notes) and sophisticated brand identity through direct reference to Renaissance art.

Process

  • Visual Element: The hand gestures were chosen as the primary motif, representing the "rebirth of art" and the creation of a fine vintage. Their placement was altered to create a unique, balanced focal point on the label, embodying the Renaissance focus on human anatomy and compositional balance.
  • Typography & Color Palette: Typography: Selection of a classic, legible serif typeface (reflecting classical inscription and order) to convey dignity and history.
  • Palette: Dominated by deep, rich colors (burgundy, black) offset by a high-impact use of gold foil (to represent "The Glass of Gold" and the premium nature of the wine), linking back to the historical use of gilt in fine art.
  • Layout: A symmetrical and ordered layout was employed, adhering to Renaissance principles of balance and harmony, ensuring all critical information (region, vintage, grape variety) is presented with clarity and proportion.

Outcome

The "Renaissance" label design successfully integrates historical artistic reference (Michelangelo's hands) with modern packaging needs. The outcome establishes a distinct, memorable, and high-quality brand identity that visually communicates the wine's rich flavor profile. The clear application of symmetrical composition and classical motif meets the requirement of contextualizing the product within the Renaissance movement.

Justice, Twisted

Conceptual Design

Overview

This project focused on the theme of moral conflict, specifically the blurring line between "right and wrong," embodied in the concept "Justice, Twisted." The objective was to translate this complex moral dilemma into two distinct and opposing conceptual fashion looks, exploring the dichotomy of purity and corruption through form, texture, and attitude. The process spanned the full design cycle, starting from thematic research and initial sketching through pattern-making, garment construction, and final styling and art direction for a professional photoshoot.

Deliverables

  • 2 Complete Fashion Looks Two fully constructed, finished garments/ensembles representing the contrasting concepts of 'Purity' and 'Corruption.'
  • Technical Documentation Design sketches, material sourcing boards (texture/color), flat drawings, and construction patterns for both looks.
  • Styling & Art Direction Defined brief for hair, makeup, and attitude to enhance the narrative.
  • Professional Photoshoot High-quality visual documentation of the final looks to capture the intended drama and conceptual tension.

Outcome

The project successfully translated abstract ethical concepts into tangible, powerful wearable art. The two final looks serve as a visual commentary on moral ambiguity, with the contrast in form and texture effectively communicating the journey from purity to corruption. The final photographic presentation (Art Direction) established the desired dramatic and challenging aesthetic, validating the designer's ability to manage a conceptual design project from initial research and sketch (Fashion Design) through to final visual execution (Art Direction and Styling).

Self Portrait 3D Model

A Renaissance-inspired wine label design using Michelangelo's The Creation of Adam hands to symbolize the wine's quality and rebirth of art.

Overview

This project involved the conceptualization and design of a premium wine label for a Moulis-en-Médoc 2006 vintage, completed between July 28 and September 2. The core concept places the product—a fine wine from the fictional vineyard "The Glass of Gold"—within the context of the Renaissance movement. The visual design is centered on an adapted motif of the iconic hand gestures from The Creation of Adam, symbolizing creation and high art. The wine's name, "Renaissance," is a deliberate wordplay referencing both the era and the designer. The overall aesthetic uses symmetrical layout, classic typography, and a rich color palette with gold foil to communicate the wine's quality and intense flavor profile (black fruits, pepper, smoke).

Outcome

The outcome is a distinct, high-end, and visually narrative wine label that successfully establishes a sophisticated brand identity for the winery's best vintage. The design differentiates the product on the shelf and enhances its perceived value by positioning it as a "rebirth of art," achieved through the clear and consistent application of Renaissance design principles like symmetry, balance, and classical motif usage.

Street Bhel Packaging Redesign

End-to-end onboarding redesign to increase activation.

Overview

This project was initiated through an on-site field assignment to identify and solve a design problem within an assigned public area. The chosen focus was the packaging used by a street Bhel vendor, a staple on-the-go snack in Mumbai. The primary design challenge was twofold: Public Health: Eliminating the use of newspaper as a food wrap, as its ink is known to contain carcinogenic substances that contaminate the food. User Experience/Sustainability: Creating a more convenient and hygienic package that maintains the on-the-go persona of the snack while utilizing cheaper, more sustainable, recycled paper materials. The redesign focused on a functional container structure that facilitates single-handed mixing and eating, replacing the traditional, messy paper cone or plate.

Outcome

The final design successfully migrated the Bhel vendor from using hazardous newspaper to cheaper, certified recycled paper, immediately eliminating the carcinogenic risk associated with ink transfer and fulfilling sustainability goals. The new ergonomic container structure enhances the snack's "on-the-go persona" by providing a hygienic, mess-free solution that significantly improves the customer experience for single-handed consumption during commuting. This scalable solution elevates both the safety and convenience standards for street food packaging.
